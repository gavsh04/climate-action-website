Accessibility Statement

We are committed to making the Climate Action website accessible to all users, regardless of ability or technology. Our goal is to provide an inclusive experience that follows the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA standards.

Implemented Features

As part of our development process, we have integrated several key accessibility features:

  • Semantic HTML: We use structural elements like <header>, <nav>, <main>, and <section> to ensure screen readers can navigate the site logically.
  • Descriptive Alt Text: All informative images, such as the solar panels and forest photography, include alt attributes to provide context for visually impaired users.
  • Color Contrast: Text colors have been chosen to maintain a high contrast ratio against backgrounds, specifically utilizing white text on dark green (#2E7D32) for primary sections.
  • Keyboard Accessibility: All interactive elements, including the navigation menu and the custom Gallery Lightbox, are reachable and operable via keyboard using the "Tab" and "Enter" keys.

Testing and Validation

To ensure these standards are met, we have utilized the following tools during development:

  • W3C Nu HTML Checker: To verify that our markup follows standard protocols.
  • WAVE (Web Accessibility Evaluation Tool): To identify and resolve potential accessibility barriers.
  • Lighthouse: For automated audits of accessibility and best practices.

Known Limitations

While we strive for full accessibility, some areas may have limitations:

  • The Interactive Simulator (AIS) involves dynamic data updates that may be complex for some assistive technologies. We are working to provide clear text-based summaries of the simulation results.
  • Some third-party elements or embedded maps may not fully support all user preferences.
