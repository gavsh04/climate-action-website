Reforestation

Restoring Earth's forests — one tree at a time — to heal our climate and ecosystems.

OVERVIEW

What Is Reforestation?

Reforestation is the process of replanting trees in areas where forests have been lost due to deforestation, wildfires, agriculture, or urban expansion. It is one of the most powerful and natural tools available for fighting climate change.

Forests cover about 31% of the Earth's land surface and are home to over 80% of terrestrial species. They act as the lungs of our planet, absorbing carbon dioxide and releasing oxygen. When forests disappear, this vital balance is disrupted — making reforestation not just beneficial, but essential.

🌍 Forests cover 31% of Earth's land
THE CRISIS

Why Forests Are Disappearing

🌾

Agriculture Expansion

Forests are cleared to make way for farmland and livestock grazing, accounting for around 80% of global deforestation.

🏗️

Urbanisation

Rapid growth of cities and infrastructure consumes forested land, fragmenting habitats and reducing biodiversity.

🪵

Illegal Logging

Timber extraction — often illegal — strips forests bare for short-term profit, causing long-term ecological damage.

🔥

Wildfires

Rising global temperatures and droughts cause more frequent and intense wildfires that destroy vast forest regions.

THE SOLUTION

How Reforestation Helps

Reforestation delivers a cascade of environmental, social, and economic benefits. Healthy forests absorb carbon from the atmosphere, regulate water cycles, prevent soil erosion, and support millions of plant and animal species. They also provide livelihoods for communities and play a critical role in maintaining clean air and water for people around the world.

KEY BENEFITS

Why It Matters

🌬️ Carbon Absorption

Trees are natural carbon sinks. A single mature tree absorbs approximately 22 kg of CO₂ per year, helping slow global warming.

🦋 Biodiversity

Forests shelter over 80% of land-based animal and plant species. Restoring them creates vital corridors for wildlife to thrive.

💧 Water Cycle Regulation

Tree roots filter and store groundwater, reduce flooding, prevent droughts, and keep rivers and streams flowing cleanly.

🌍 Soil Protection

Tree roots bind soil and prevent erosion. Reforested land recovers from degradation and becomes productive again over time.

GLOBAL ACTION

Reforestation Efforts Around the World

Governments, NGOs, and communities worldwide are committing to large-scale tree planting initiatives. Ethiopia planted over 350 million trees in a single day in 2019. China's "Great Green Wall" project has restored millions of hectares of degraded land. The UN's Trillion Tree Campaign encourages individuals and countries to plant, restore, and conserve trees globally. These efforts show that collective action can make a real difference.

MILESTONES

Key Global Reforestation Milestones

Year Event Description
1978 China's Great Green Wall China launches a massive shelterbelts project to combat desertification — now the world's largest reforestation programme.
2006 UNEP Billion Tree Campaign The United Nations Environment Programme launches a campaign that eventually grows into the Trillion Tree initiative.
2011 Bonn Challenge A global initiative to restore 350 million hectares of deforested and degraded land by 2030 is launched in Germany.
2019 Ethiopia's Record Day Ethiopia plants 353 million trees in a single day as part of its Green Legacy initiative — a world record at the time.
2021 COP26 Forest Pledge Over 100 world leaders sign the Glasgow Leaders' Declaration on Forests and Land Use, pledging to end deforestation by 2030.
TAKE ACTION

How You Can Help

01

Plant a Tree

Join a local tree planting event or plant a native tree in your garden, school, or neighbourhood.

02

Support Reforestation Charities

Donate to organisations like Trees for the Future, One Tree Planted, or the Eden Reforestation Projects.

03

Reduce Paper Use

Go digital where possible. Less demand for paper reduces the pressure to log forests for pulp and timber.

04

Choose Sustainable Products

Look for FSC-certified wood and paper products that come from responsibly managed forests.

🌳

Conclusion

Reforestation is one of the most effective and accessible climate solutions available today. Every tree planted is a vote for a healthier planet. By working together — as individuals, communities, and nations — we can restore the forests our world depends on and secure a sustainable future for generations to come.
